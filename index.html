Kerry Dunnington:
Chef and Author

Kerry Dunnington, a culinary consultant and caterer, specializes in healthy eating and entertaining.

In addition to writing Tasting the Seasons and her earlier book, she writes a monthly column, Tasting the Seasons for Slow Food. She has also written for regional and national magazines and newspapers including the Examiner newspapers, Smart Woman, Baltimore Eats, and the Urbanite.

Dunnington frequently does health-oriented cooking presentations on sustainability for Slow Food, Whole Foods Market and Bon App√©tit Management Company for Johns Hopkins University students and faculty.

A life-long eco-stylist and food enthusiast, Dunnington has served on the Chef's Council and the Board of the American Institute of Wine and Food and is founder of Slow Food Baltimore's "Eat in Season Challenge."

In addition to endorsing and encouraging sustainable practices, Kerry enthusiastically shares the benefits of bioregional living. Dunnington's new cookbook, This Book Cooks will be published in June 2015.

The author lives with her husband (and official taste-tester), Nick, and their Norwich terrier, Caramel, in Baltimore, Maryland. Kerry is at work on her third cookbook.

This Book Cooks!

With a strong nod toward the farm-fresh approach This Book Cooks reveals a varied collection of nearly 200 classic, clearly written, and extremely reliable recipes. Helpful cooking techniques and inspiring introductory material are further enhanced by stories drawn from Kerry's life experiences in cooking/entertaining.

A comprehensive index and a stylish, user-friendly format make it completely accessible, easy to navigate, and an inspiring cover-to-cover culinary read. Set up traditionally and divided into nine chapters from appetizers to desserts, the book is designed for real people who like real food cooking in real kitchens.

In keeping with her ongoing efforts to champion ecologically sound, food related messages, the cookbook also includes three eco-friendly, family-oriented educational sections
